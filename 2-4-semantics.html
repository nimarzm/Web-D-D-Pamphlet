Semmantic tags

Header [block] tag

A group of introductory or navigational aids: title, navigation links, etc. In fact, this is the links above each university website, that shows almost all the sections of the website (navigational).

We can use <nav> tag to link to other pages. This tag is mainly embeded in the header tag. We can also use lists in defining our nav tag.

Footer [block] tag

In this section, we mainly insert info such as copyright data, related documents, link to social media and etc. This is typically at the bottom of the page, but it is not require because it is footer, we should insert it in th foot of the site!

Figure [block] tag

With this block tag, we are able to give multiple meaning to a media, than a simple <img> tag. we can add:

  1. Caption
  2. Multiple multimedia resources

The semantic tags (footer, header, nav, etc). do not affect the placement of your elements. Instead they provide some context to users who may be accessing your page through non-visual means.
